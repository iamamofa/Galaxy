Center for Genomic Epidemiology (CGE)

CGE provides web-based tools for bacterial genome analysis, phenotyping, typing, phylogeny, and metagenomics. These services help identify antimicrobial resistance, virulence genes, plasmids, and bacterial species.

Phenotyping Tools

  • ResFinder: Identify acquired antibiotic resistance genes
  • ResFinderFG: Identify functional metagenomic resistance determinants
  • LRE-finder: Detect genes/mutations causing linezolid resistance
  • KmerResistance: Resistance gene detection using k-mers
  • PathogenFinder: Predict bacterial pathogenicity towards humans
  • VirulenceFinder: Identify acquired virulence genes
  • SPIFinder, ToxFinder, Restriction-ModificationFinder

Typing Tools

  • MLST and cgMLSTFinder: Multi-locus sequence typing for genomes or reads
  • PlasmidFinder and pMLST: Plasmid detection and typing
  • KmerFinder and SpeciesFinder: Species prediction
  • SeroTypeFinder, SeqSero, spaTyper, FimTyper, CHTyper, PAst, SCCmecFinder

Phylogeny Tools

  • MINTyper, CSIPhylogeny, NDtree: SNP-based phylogenetic analysis
  • Evergreen: Forest of updated phylogenetic trees from public bacterial genomes
  • TreeViewer: Visualization of phylogenetic trees

Metagenomics Tools

  • CCMetagen: Identification of eukaryotes and prokaryotes in metagenomes
  • RUCS: Rapid identification of PCR primer pairs for unique sequences

Other Tools

  • MyKMAfinder, MyDbFinder, MyKmerFinder: Typing/phenotyping using custom databases
  • DeHumanizer: Human read filtering
  • MetaPhinder: Identify bacteriophage sequences
  • Flankophile: Gene synteny analysis
