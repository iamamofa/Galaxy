Cholera Case Study

Introduction

In this final module, you'll apply everything you've learned to analyze real Cholera samples. We'll use Illumina sequencing data from actual outbreak samples to perform a complete genomic analysis.

Vibrio cholerae - the causative agent of cholera

About Vibrio cholerae

Vibrio cholerae is a Gram-negative bacterium that causes cholera, a severe diarrheal disease. Understanding its genome helps us:

  • Track outbreak sources and transmission
  • Identify antibiotic resistance genes
  • Understand virulence factors
  • Develop better prevention strategies

Analysis Workflow

Step 1: Quality Control

Use FastQC to assess the quality of your Illumina reads. Look for:

  • Per base sequence quality
  • Adapter contamination
  • Over-represented sequences

Step 2: Read Trimming

Use Trimmomatic or fastp to remove low-quality bases and adapters

Step 3: Genome Assembly

Assemble the reads using SPAdes or MEGAHIT to reconstruct the genome

Step 4: Assembly Quality Assessment

Use QUAST to evaluate assembly quality:

  • Number of contigs
  • N50 value
  • Total assembly length

Step 5: Genome Annotation

Annotate genes using Prokka to identify:

  • Protein-coding genes
  • RNA genes
  • Functional annotations

Step 6: Comparative Analysis

Compare your samples to identify:

  • SNPs and variants
  • Antibiotic resistance genes
  • Virulence factors
  • Phylogenetic relationships

Expected Outcomes

By the end of this case study, you will have:

  • High-quality assembled genomes
  • Annotated gene lists
  • Identification of key virulence and resistance genes
  • Phylogenetic tree showing relationships between samples
  • A complete Galaxy workflow that can be reused

Dataset: Remember that all samples are available at Zenodo. Make sure you've created your paired collection as described in the Galaxy Collections module before proceeding with the analysis.
